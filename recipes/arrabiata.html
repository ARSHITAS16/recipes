ARRABIATA PASTA

DESCRIPTION

Arrabbiata pasta, or “angry” pasta, is a spicy and flavorful Italian dish straight from the heart of Rome! Made with simple ingredients like garlic, tomatoes, olive oil, and red chili flakes, this sauce packs a fiery kick. Tossed with penne pasta, it’s the perfect blend of heat and rich tomato goodness, making it a go-to comfort food for spice lovers

Originally popularized in Roman trattorias, arrabbiata pasta has won hearts worldwide for its bold and zesty taste. Whether you like it extra spicy or with a sprinkle of Parmesan and fresh herbs, this classic dish is easy to make and impossible to resist. One bite, and you'll see why it’s an all-time Italian favorite!

INGREDIENTS

  • 200 g penne pasta
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3-4 garlic cloves(minced)
  • 1 tsp red chilli flakes
  • 1 cup canned or fresh tomatoes
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley or basil(chopped)
  • Grated parmesan cheese

STEPS

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil water in a large pot, add salt, and cook the penne until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving a little pasta water.
  2. Prepare the sauce:Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the heat:Stir in red chili flakes and cook for a few more seconds. Then, add the chopped or pureed tomatoes.
  4. Season it:Mix in salt, black pepper, sugar (if using), and oregano. Let the sauce simmer for 5-7 minutes until it thickens.
  5. Toss the pasta:Add the cooked penne to the sauce, tossing well to coat. If needed, add a splash of reserved pasta water for a silky texture.
  6. Garnish and serve:Sprinkle with fresh parsley or basil, and top with grated Parmesan if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!
TIPFor extra spice, add more chili flakes or a dash of chili powder!
